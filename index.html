Directness Mastery | 17 Key Phrases

Speak Your Mind, Take Action

A powerful collection of phrases for encouraging explicit communication, demanding honesty, and pushing for decisive action in the workplace.

Series 1: Demanding Clarity (Phrases 1-4)

The Call for Content

  • What are you trying to say? [O que você está tentando dizer?] A direct request for clarification when communication is vague or indirect. *I'm still unclear on the conclusion; **what are you trying to say**?*
  • Say what you mean, and mean what you say. [Diga o que quer dizer e seja coerente.] A strong encouragement for honesty and consistency in speech. *In high-stakes meetings, you must **say what you mean, and mean what you say**.*

Encouraging Expression

  • If you've got something to say, say it. [Se você tem algo a dizer, diga.] Used to prompt a hesitant speaker to voice their opinion or concern. *Please don't hold back; **if you've got something to say, say it**.*
  • If that's how it is, just say so. [Se é assim, diga logo.] A way to ask for confirmation of a reality, especially a negative one, without ambiguity. *If the project deadline is impossible, **just say so**.*

Series 2: Avoiding Ambiguity and Evasion (Phrases 5-8)

Tough Talk

  • Don't be mealy-mouthed. [Não seja evasivo.] Do not be afraid to say what you really think; avoid sounding weak or uncertain. *When negotiating, **don't be mealy-mouthed** about your price.*
  • Don't mince your words. [Não meça as palavras / Fale sem receio.] To speak clearly and frankly, without softening the message. *I need to know the risks, so please **don't mince your words**.*

Honesty Required

  • Tell it like it is. [Fale sem rodeios / Conte a verdade nua e crua.] A plea for a brutally honest, unvarnished account or assessment. *The pitch failed badly. **Tell it like it is**, what went wrong?*
  • Be straightforward. / Be straight with me. [Seja direto. / Seja sincero comigo.] Asking for directness, honesty, and transparency. *Look, if the budget is cut, **be straight with me**.*

Series 3: Action, Resilience, and Openness (Phrases 9-12)

Initiative

  • Let's do something about it. / We have to do something about this. [Vamos fazer algo sobre isso.] A call to move from discussion/complaint to concrete steps and solutions. *Complaining won't help. **Let's do something about it**.*
  • When the going gets tough, the tough get going. [Quando as coisas apertam, os fortes seguem em frente.] An idiom celebrating resilience and determination in the face of difficulty. *The market is down, but **when the going gets tough, the tough get going**.*

Complete Disclosure

  • Let's get everything out in the open. [Vamos colocar tudo às claras.] A request to reveal all facts, issues, and concerns, ensuring nothing is hidden. *Before signing, **let's get everything out in the open**.*
  • One thing at a time. [Uma coisa de cada vez.] A phrase used to manage an excessive workload or to maintain focus by tackling tasks sequentially. *We have three problems to solve, but let's take **one thing at a time**.*

Series 4: Timing, Pace, and Missteps (Phrases 13-17)

Positive Pace

  • We're right on schedule. / We're making good time. [Estamos dentro do cronograma / Estamos avançando bem.] Positive updates indicating work is progressing as planned or faster than expected. *Good news, the implementation is solid; **we're right on schedule**.*
  • Time is on our side. [O tempo está a nosso favor.] Meaning there is enough time, or the delay actually benefits our position. *Since the competitor is having issues, **time is on our side**.*

Verbal Mistakes

  • Put one's foot in one's mouth. [Dar um fora / Falar o que não devia.] To accidentally say something embarrassing or tactless. *I really **put my foot in my mouth** when I called the CEO by the wrong name.*
  • Shoot yourself in the foot. [Dar um tiro no próprio pé.] To unintentionally do or say something that harms your own cause or position. *He **shot himself in the foot** by insulting the interviewer.*

Bonus Phrase

  • This is to say that we can make time good. [Isso quer dizer que podemos otimizar o tempo.] A more formal way of saying we can improve the schedule or manage the time efficiently. *If we automate this task, **this is to say that we can make time good**.*
